Most tradies lose jobs before the phone ever rings. A customer finds you on a phone, cannot quickly see your work or tap to call, and rings the next tradie instead. A single profile page fixes that: photos, reviews, your service area, and tap-to-call and WhatsApp buttons in one place, so a viewer becomes a call in one tap instead of giving up.

The job is rarely lost on skill. It is lost on friction. Here is where that friction hides and how a profile page removes it.

## Where tradies leak enquiries

Picture how someone actually hires a tradie today. They are standing in a leaking bathroom, on their phone, and they want certainty fast. They lose interest at every extra step:

- Your Facebook page has no clear phone number near the top.
- Your reviews are scattered or hard to find.
- They have to copy your mobile number into their dialler by hand.
- They cannot tell if you cover their suburb.

Every one of those is a moment to give up and call the next name on the list. Around six in ten Google searches now happen on a mobile, and a phone screen is small and impatient. The tradie who is easiest to contact often wins, not the best one.

## What a profile page does differently

A trade profile page is one link that holds everything a customer needs to decide, laid out for a thumb on a phone:

- A **profile photo** and a plain-English line on what you do and where.
- **Photos of recent work**, including before-and-after shots that show your standard.
- **Customer reviews** in one place, so trust is built on the spot.
- Your **availability**, so they know you can actually take the job.
- Big **tap-to-call and WhatsApp buttons** that start the contact in a single tap.

The buttons are the whole point. Everything above them builds trust. The buttons turn that trust into a phone call before the customer cools off or gets distracted.

## Fewer taps means more calls

The simplest way to get more enquiries is not more traffic, it is less friction on the traffic you already have. Every tap between "this tradie looks good" and "I'm calling them" loses a share of people. Cut the journey from five taps to one, and more of the same viewers turn into actual calls.

That is why a dedicated page beats a buried phone number or a busy social profile. It is built for one job: get the visitor to contact you, now.

## One link to share everywhere

A profile page is also one link you can put everywhere you already show up:

- In your **Instagram and Facebook bio**.
- On your **van signage** and business cards (as a short link or QR code).
- In your **email signature** and quote replies.
- In **local Facebook group** comments when someone asks for a recommendation.

## Make it effortless to hire you

You cannot control how many people stumble across your work, but you can control what happens when they do. Give them one clear page, your best photos, your reviews, and a button that calls you in a single tap. Remove the friction and the same number of viewers turns into noticeably more booked jobs.
