A single trade link beats a Facebook page or a buried phone number for one reason: it is built to convert a viewer into a call, not to entertain them. One page holds your work, your reviews, your service area and tap-to-call buttons, with no feed to scroll past. Share that one link everywhere and a bigger share of the people who see you actually contact you.

More work does not always mean more eyeballs. Often it means converting the eyeballs you already have. Here is why one link does that better than the alternatives.

## The problem with how most tradies show up online

Most tradies are scattered across places that were never built to win jobs:

- A **Facebook page** built for posts and likes, where your phone number is three taps deep.
- An **Instagram grid** of great work with no obvious way to hire you.
- A **phone number** on a van that people have to memorise at a red light.
- **Word of mouth**, where every new lead means retyping your details in a chat.

None of these is bad. But each one adds steps between "I like this tradie" and "I'm calling them", and every step quietly loses people. The work is there. The conversion is not.

## What "one link" actually means

A single trade link is one web address that sends people to a page built for a single job: getting them to contact you. On that page, a customer can:

- See **who you are** and **what you do** in seconds.
- Scroll your **best photos** and **before-and-after** work.
- Read your **reviews** without leaving the page.
- Check you cover their **area** and that you are **available**.
- Tap **call** or **WhatsApp** to reach you immediately.

It works like a link in bio, but tuned for tradies instead of influencers. The whole page points at one outcome: a phone call.

## Why one link converts better

Three things make a dedicated link out-pull a social page or a bare number:

1. **No distractions.** There is no feed to scroll, no other posts, no rabbit hole. Just your work and a way to hire you.
2. **Trust and action in one place.** Reviews and photos build confidence, and the call button is right there to act on it before the customer cools off.
3. **One tap to contact.** Tap-to-call and WhatsApp remove the most common drop-off point, which is making someone type your number by hand.

Cut the path from five steps to one and the same traffic produces more calls. That is the entire game.

## Put your one link everywhere

The other win is repetition. One link replaces a hundred copy-and-paste introductions:

- Drop it in your **Instagram, Facebook and TikTok bio**.
- Add it as a **QR code** on your van, business cards and quotes.
- Paste it into **local Facebook groups** when someone asks for a recommendation.
- Put it in your **email signature** and text it to new leads.

Every place you already appear becomes a doorway to the same high-converting page. You stop selling yourself from scratch each time and let one link do it for you.
